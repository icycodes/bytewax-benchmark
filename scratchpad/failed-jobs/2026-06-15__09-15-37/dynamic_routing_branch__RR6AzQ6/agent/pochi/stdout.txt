### How it works

1. **`FileSource`** reads `sensors.json` line-by-line.
2. **`op.map("parse", ...)`** runs `parse_line` on every line, returning a `("error"|"valid", payload)` tagged tuple.
   - Invalid JSON → `("error", '{"error": "invalid json", "raw": "..."}')` 
   - Missing `sensor_type` → `("error", '{"error": "missing sensor_type", "raw": "..."}')` 
   - Otherwise → `("valid", <dict>)`
3. **First `op.branch`** (`split_errors`) divides the stream into `.trues` (errors) and `.falses` (valid records).
4. **Second `op.branch`** (`split_temp`) divides valid records into `.trues` (temperature) and `.falses` (humidity).
5. `celsius_to_fahrenheit` pops `value_c`, adds `value_f = value_c * 9/5 + 32`, and serialises to a JSON string.
6. All three streams are keyed with `op.key_on` (required by `FileSink`'s `FixedPartitionedSink` contract) and written to their respective output files.
7. The `if __name__ == "__main__": run_main(flow)` guard lets `python dataflow.py` execute the dataflow directly.
