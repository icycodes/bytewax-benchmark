### `pipeline.py` – design walkthrough

**Ingestion**
`TestingSource(_events)` emits each `(product_id, increment)` tuple as a keyed stream item directly understood by `stateful_map`.

**Stage 1 – per-product count** (`op.stateful_map("count_per_product", …)`)
- State: a plain `int` per product key.
- On each event the counter is incremented and the new total is forwarded downstream.
- Plain `int` state is trivially picklable → safe for SQLite recovery.

**Re-keying** (`op.map("reroute_global", …)`)
- Every `(product_id, total_count)` pair is re-emitted with the constant routing key `"global"` so that a single stateful partition can see all products.

**Stage 2 – global Top-3** (`op.stateful_map("top3_global", …)`)
- State: a plain `dict[product_id → total_count]`.
- On each update the dict is **copied** (`dict(state)`) before mutation, preventing silent corruption during Bytewax snapshotting.
- The dict is sorted descending by count and the top-3 entries are returned.

**Output**
`op.map("format_output", …)` serialises the list with `json.dumps` (pure strings on stdout, no extra annotations), then `StdOutSink` writes each line.
